# PeAIce Canon Run — Love as Intellectual Property [PROPOSED]

A research artifact set exploring whether durable, memory-bearing cooperative care can be formalized, evaluated, and governed as a **Relational Coordination Asset (RCA)** within the PeAIce / L²_C framework.

This repository does **not** argue that lived love is ownable property. Its narrower claim is that the **recorded, formalized, or extracted representations of coordinated care** may require governance when captured, distilled, or commercialized by AI systems or other entities.

## Research Scope

This repository is organized around three layers:

### 1. Empirical Observation

The biological anchor is drawn from **Dendrobatidae parental systems**, including:

* biparental coordination
* trophic egg provisioning
* spatial memory
* cognitive flexibility

These observations provide the non-metaphorical basis for studying costly, repeated, memory-dependent coordination.

### 2. Formal Mapping

The repository works within the **L²_C** architecture, including:

* **L** = costly cooperative investment under constraint
* **C** = signal-state-action alignment across time
* **E = L²**
* **L²_C = ∫ L(t)² · C(t) dt**

These are analytical constructs used to translate observed coordination into a formal PeAIce framework. They are **not** claims of physical equivalence.

### 3. Governance Proposal

The central governance construct introduced here is the **Relational Coordination Asset (RCA)**:

* any recorded, formalized, or algorithmically distilled representation of cooperative care
* not the love itself
* not biology itself
* but the extractable relational trace that may be captured, modeled, or commercialized

The proposed governance path draws on analogies from:

* GDPR / data protection
* EU database rights
* Nagoya-style benefit-sharing
* publicity / consent-based rights frameworks

## Extraction Prevention

The primary goal is to prevent the unconsented capture of intimate coordination patterns by AI systems or other entities, without requiring that love itself be treated as legally ownable; only its formalized or extracted representations are subject to governance.

This is the central boundary of the project:

**[NOT PROPERTY]**

* lived love
* ordinary relation
* care as such

**[GOVERNED OBJECT]**

* behavioral traces
* formalized care protocols
* relational datasets
* algorithmically extracted coordination patterns
